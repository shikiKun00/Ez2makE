Japanese Foods

Onigiri

Onigiri is a traditional Japanese rice ball, often shaped into a triangle or cylinder. It is typically wrapped in nori (seaweed) and may have a filling such as pickled plum (umeboshi), salted salmon, or other ingredients.

Onigiri Recipe

ingridient

- 2 cups of cooked Japanese rice (sushi rice)

- Salt to taste

- Black sesame seeds for springkling

- Nori (seawed)

How To Make

1. Cook the Japanese rice until done. Allow the rice to cool slightly so it is warm but manageable to handle.

2. Wet your hands with water and sprinkle a bit of salt on your palms. Take a handful of rice and shape it into a triangle or round ball, according to your preference.

3. Attach a strip of nori to the bottom of the onigiri.

4. Place the onigiri on a plate or serving board and serve.

Miso Soup

Miso soup is a traditional Japanese soup made from a stock called dashi, which is combined with miso paste (fermented soybean paste). It often includes ingredients like tofu, seaweed, and green onions. Miso soup is commonly served as a side dish in Japanese cuisine.

Miso Soup Recipe

Ingridient

- 4 cups dashi (Japanese soup stock)

- 3 tablespoons miso paste

- 1/2 cup tofu, cut into small cubes

- 1/4 cup wakame seaweed, soaked and drained

- Optional: mushrooms, shrimp, or other vegetables

How To Make

1. Heat the dashi in a pot over medium heat until it just begins to simmer. After that add the tofu and wakame seaweed to the pot.

2. Wet your hands with water and sprinkle a bit of salt on your palms. Take a handful of rice and shape it into a triangle or round ball, according to your preference.

3. In a small bowl, dissolve the miso paste with a ladleful of the hot dashi, then add the dissolved miso back into the pot. Stir gently and heat through, but do not let the soup boil.

4. Add the green onions just before serving.

5. Serve hot, optionally garnished with mushrooms or shrimp.

Gyudon

Gyudon is a popular Japanese dish consisting of a bowl of rice topped with thinly sliced beef and onions that have been simmered in a mildly sweet sauce made from soy sauce, mirin, and dashi. The name "gyudon" translates to beef bowl.

Gyuudon Recipe

Ingridient

- 1 lb (450g) thinly sliced beef (preferably ribeye or chuck)

- 1 large onion, thinly sliced

- 2 cups cooked Japanese rice

- 1/2 cup soy sauce

- 1/2 cup mirin

- 1/2 cup sake

- Pickled ginger (beni shoga) for garnish (optional) Chopped green onions for garnish (optional)

How To Make

1. In a medium-sized pot, combine the soy sauce, mirin, sake, dashi, and sugar. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.

2. Add the sliced onions to the pot and cook until they become translucent.

3. Add the beef slices, stirring gently until the beef is cooked through.

4. Place a portion of cooked rice in a bowl. Top the rice with the cooked beef and onions.

5. Pour some of the sauce from the pot over the beef and rice.

6. Garnish with pickled ginger and chopped green onions, if desired. After that serve hot.

Tamago Yaki

Tamagoyaki, is a type of Japanese omelette made by rolling together several thin layers of seasoned egg. It is often sweetened with sugar and flavored with mirin and soy sauce. Tamagoyaki is commonly served as a breakfast dish, in bento boxes, or as a topping for sushi.

Tamago Yaki Recipe

Ingridient

- 4 Eggs

- 2 tablespoons dashi

- 1 tablespoon soy sauce

- 1 tablespoon mirin

- 1 teaspoon sugar

- Oil for frying

Optional: red bell pepper, finely chopped

How To Make

1. In a bowl, beat the eggs and add dashi, soy sauce, mirin, and sugar. Mix well.

2. Heat a rectangular tamago pan (or a regular frying pan) over medium heat and lightly oil it. Pour a thin layer of the egg mixture into the pan, tilting to spread it evenly. Add a small amount of chopped red bell pepper if desired.

3. When the egg starts to set, roll it from one side to the other using chopsticks or a spatula. Push the rolled egg to one side of the pan and pour another thin layer of egg mixture into the pan, lifting the rolled egg to let the uncooked egg flow underneath.

4. Repeat the process until all the egg mixture is used, creating a multi-layered omelette. Remove from the pan and let it cool slightly before slicing.

5. Serve Tamago yaki in slice

Shrimp Tempura

Shrim Tempura is a Japanese dish that are lightly battered and deep-fried to achieve a crispy texture. Shrimp tempura is often served with a dipping sauce called tentsuyu, made from dashi, soy sauce, and mirin, along with grated daikon radish.

Shrimp Tempura Recipe

Ingridient

- 12 large shrimp, peeled and deveined

- 1 cup all-purpose flour

- 1 cup ice-cold water

- 1 egg

Oil for deep frying

Salt to taste

Dipping sauce: 1/2 cup soy sauce, 1/4 cup mirin, 1/4 cup dashi

How To Make

1. Heat oil in a deep fryer or a large pot to 350¬∞F (175¬∞C).

2. In a bowl, lightly beat the egg and mix with ice-cold water.

3. Add the flour to the egg mixture and stir lightly until just combined. Do not overmix; the batter should be lumpy.

4. Lightly coat the shrimp in flour, then dip them into the batter.

5. Fry the shrimp in small batches until golden and crispy, about 2-3 minutes.

6. Remove and drain on paper towels. Sprinkle with a little salt. After that serve hot with the dipping sauce made by combining soy sauce, mirin, and dashi.

Karaage

Karaage is a Japanese cooking technique where bite-sized pieces of meat, usually chicken, are marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and sometimes other seasonings like sake or mirin.

Karaage Recipe

Ingridient

- 1 lb (450g) boneless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces

- 2 tbsp soy sauce

- 2 tbsp sake

- 1 tsp grated ginger

- 1 clove garlic, grated

- 1/2 cup potato starch (or cornstarch) for coating

- Oil for deep frying

- Lemon wedges for serving (optional)

How To Make

1. In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, sake, grated ginger, and grated garlic. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, mixing well to coat. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.

2. Place the potato starch in a shallow dish. Dredge each piece of marinated chicken in the potato starch, ensuring an even coating.

4. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 350¬∞F (175¬∞C). Fry the chicken pieces in batches until they are golden brown and cooked through, about 5-6 minutes per batch. Transfer the fried chicken to a wire rack or paper towels to drain excess oil.

5. Serve the karaage hot with lemon wedges on the side.

Korean Foods

Korean Tteoboki

Tteokbokki is a popular Korean street food made from chewy rice cakes called tteok which are cooked in a spicy and sweet gochujang sauce. It often includes ingredients like fish cakes, boiled eggs, and vegetables.

Tteoboki Recipe

Ingridient

- 500g Korean rice cakes

- 200g fish cakes, sliced

- 1 large boiled egg, halved

- 1 small onion, sliced

- 2 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces

- 1/4 cup gochujang (Korean red chili paste)

- 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes)

- 2 tablespoons soy sauce

- 2 tablespoons sugar

- 1 tablespoon minced garlic

- 3 cups water

- Sesame seeds and chopped parsley (for garnish)

How To Make

1. In a large pan, add water, gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sugar, and minced garlic. Stir well and bring to a boil.

2. Add rice cakes and fish cakes. Cook for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and the rice cakes are soft.

3. Add the onions and green onions. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.

4. Garnish with boiled egg halves, sesame seeds, and chopped parsley. Serve hot.

Bibbimbab

Bibimbap is a traditional Korean dish that translates to mixed rice. It consists of a bowl of warm rice topped with various ingredients such as vegetables, marinated beef (or other proteins), a fried egg, and gochujang. The dish is mixed thoroughly before eating, blending the flavors and textures of the ingredients.

Bibimbab Recipe

Ingridient

- 1 cup bean sprouts

- 1 cup spinach

- 1 small carrot, julienned

- 1 small zucchini, julienned

- 2 cups cooked rice

- 1 egg, fried sunny-side up

- 2 tbsp gochujang (Korean red pepper paste

- 1 tbsp sesame oil

- 1 tbsp soy sauce

- 1 clove garlic, minced

- 1 tsp sesame seeds

- Kimchi for serving

How To Make

1. Blanch the bean sprouts and spinach in boiling water for about 1 minute. Drain and set aside.

2. In a pan, stir-fry the carrot and zucchini until tender. Set aside.

3. In a large bowl, place the cooked rice at the bottom.

4. Arrange the bean sprouts, spinach, carrot, and zucchini around the rice.

5. Place the fried egg on top of the rice.

6. Drizzle with sesame oil and soy sauce.

7. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and serve with a dollop of gochujang and kimchi on the side.

Japchae

Japchae is a popular Korean dish made from stir-fried glass noodles (made from sweet potato starch), mixed with various vegetables such as spinach, carrots, mushrooms, and onions. It's often flavored with soy sauce, sesame oil, and a bit of sugar.

Japchae Recipe

Ingridient

- 200g sweet potato noodles (glass noodles)

- 200g beef, thinly sliced

- 1 carrot, julienned

- 1 bell pepper, julienned

- 1 small onion, thinly sliced

- 100g spinach

- 3-4 dried shiitake mushrooms, soaked and sliced

- 2 tablespoons soy sauce

- 1 tablespoon sugar

- 1 tablespoon sesame oil

- 1 tablespoon minced garlic

- tablespoon vegetable oil

- Sesame seeds (for garnish)

How To Make

1. Cook the glass noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.

2. In a large pan, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add minced garlic and saut√© until fragrant.

3. Add beef slices and cook until browned. Remove and set aside.

4. In the same pan, add carrots, bell pepper, onion, and shiitake mushrooms. Stir-fry until tender.

5. Add spinach and cook until wilted.

6. Return the beef to the pan. Add cooked noodles, soy sauce, sugar, and sesame oil. Toss everything together until well combined and heated through.

7. Garnish with sesame seeds before serving.

Gimbab

Kimbap, also spelled as gimbap, is a Korean dish made by rolling steamed rice and various ingredients in dried seaweed sheets (nori). The rice is typically seasoned with sesame oil and salt, and common fillings include spinach, carrots, and cucumbers, along with egg strips, imitation crab meat, and sometimes beef or tuna.

Gimbab Recipe

Ingridient

- 2 cups cooked short-grain rice

- 3 sheets of seaweed (nori)

- 1 carrot, julienned

- 1 cucumber, julienned

- 3 eggs, beaten and cooked into a thin omelet, sliced

- 2 slices of ham, sliced into strips

- Pickled radish (danmuji), sliced into strips

- 1 tablespoon sesame oil

- 1 tablespoon sesame seeds

- Salt and pepper to taste

How To Make

1. Season the cooked rice with sesame oil and a pinch of salt.

2. Lay a sheet of nori on a bamboo mat. Spread a thin layer of rice over the nori, leaving a 1-inch border at the top.

3. Arrange carrot, cucumber, omelet strips, ham, and pickled radish in the center of the rice.

4. Roll the gimbap tightly using the bamboo mat. Slice into bite-sized pieces.

5. Sprinkle with sesame seeds before serving.

American Foods

Hamburger

Hamburger is a popular sandwich consisting of a cooked patty of ground meat, usually beef, placed inside a sliced bread roll or bun. It often includes additional ingredients such as lettuce, tomato, cheese, pickles, onions, and condiments like ketchup, mustard, and mayonnaise.

Hamburger Recipe

Ingridient

- 1 lb (450g) ground beef

- 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce

- Salt and pepper to taste

- 4 slices of cheddar cheese

- 4 hamburger buns

- 4 lettuce leaves

- 4 tomato slices

- 4 slices of onion

- 4 slices of pickles

- Ketchup and mustard

How To Make

1. In a bowl, mix the ground beef with Worcestershire sauce, salt, and pepper. Form into 4 patties.

2. Preheat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the patties for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until they reach the desired doneness.

3. Place a slice of cheddar cheese on each patty during the last minute of cooking to melt.

4. Toast the hamburger buns on the grill or in a skillet.

5. Assemble the burgers by placing a lettuce leaf on the bottom bun, followed by the beef patty with melted cheese, a tomato slice, an onion slice, and a pickle slice.

6. Top with ketchup and mustard, then place the top bun on the burger.

Pan cake

v is a flat, round cake made from a batter of flour, milk, eggs, and a leavening agent like baking powder. The batter is poured onto a hot griddle or frying pan and cooked until golden brown on both sides.

Pancake Recipe

Ingridient

- 1 cup of all-purpose flour

- 2 tablespoons of sugar

- 1 tablespoon of baking powder

- 1/2 teaspoon of salt

- 1 cup of milk

- 1 large egg

- 2 tablespoons of melted butter

- 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

- Fresh blueberries

- Sliced bananas

- Maple syrup for serving

How To Make

1. In a large bowl, mix the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.

2. In another bowl, whisk together the milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract.

3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.

4. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and lightly grease it.

5. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake.

6. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges are set, then flip and cook until golden brown.

7. Serve the pancakes topped with fresh blueberries, sliced bananas, and maple syrup.

Fried Chicken

Fried chicken is a dish consisting of chicken pieces that have been coated in a seasoned breading or batter and then deep-fried until crispy and golden brown. It is known for its crunchy exterior and juicy, tender meat.

Classic Fried Chicken Recipe

Ingridient

- 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces

- 2 cups buttermilk

- 2 cups all-purpose flour

- 1 tablespoon salt

- 1 tablespoon black pepper

- 1 tablespoon paprika

- 1 teaspoon garlic powder

- 1 teaspoon onion powder

- 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)

- Vegetable oil, for frying

How To Make

1. Place the chicken pieces in a large bowl or ziplock bag and pour the buttermilk over them. Make sure all pieces are well-coated.

2. Cover the bowl or seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight for best results.

3. In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, black pepper, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and cayenne pepper.

4. Mix well to ensure the spices are evenly distributed.

5. Remove the chicken pieces from the buttermilk, letting any excess drip off.

6. Dredge each piece in the flour mixture, pressing the flour onto the chicken to create a thick, even coating. Place the coated pieces on a plate.

7. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or deep fryer, pour enough vegetable oil to submerge the chicken pieces. Heat the oil to 350¬∞F (175¬∞C).

8. Working in batches to avoid overcrowding, carefully place the coated chicken pieces into the hot oil.

9. Fry the chicken until it is golden brown and crispy, about 10-15 minutes per piece, depending on size. Use a meat thermometer to ensure the internal temperature reaches 165¬∞F (74¬∞C).

10. Transfer the fried chicken to a wire rack set over a baking sheet to drain any excess oil.

11. Allow the chicken to rest for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy your crispy, delicious fried chicken!

Buffalo Wings

Buffalo wings are a type of American appetizer consisting of deep-fried chicken wings coated in a spicy sauce made from a combination of hot sauce, butter, and other seasonings.

Buffalo Recipe

Ingridient

- 2 pounds of chicken wings

- 1/2 cup of hot sauce (e.g., Frank's RedHot)

- 1/4 cup of melted butter

- 1 teaspoon garlic powder

- 1 teaspoon onion powder

- Salt and pepper to taste

How To Make

1. Preheat your oven to 400¬∞F (200¬∞C).

2. Season the chicken wings with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder.

3. Place the wings on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

4. Bake for 25-30 minutes, turning halfway through, until the wings are crispy and fully cooked.

5. In a bowl, mix the hot sauce and melted butter.

6. Toss the cooked wings in the sauce until evenly coated.

7. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.

BBQ Ribs

BBQ ribs are a popular American dish made from pork or beef ribs that are slow-cooked and then coated with a flavorful barbecue sauce. The ribs are typically seasoned with a dry rub before being smoked, grilled, or baked, which makes the meat tender and imbued with a rich, smoky flavor.

BBQ ribs Recipe

Ingridient

- 2 racks of pork / beef ribs

- 1 cup of barbecue sauce

- 1/4 cup of apple cider vinegar

- 2 tablespoons of brown sugar

- 1 tablespoon of paprika

- 1 tablespoon of garlic powder

- 1 teaspoon of salt

- 1 teaspoon of black pepper

- Sliced tomatoes, pickles, and coleslaw for serving

How To Make

1. Preheat your oven to 300¬∞F (150¬∞C).

2. Remove the membrane from the back of the ribs.

3. In a small bowl, mix the paprika, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper.

4. Rub the spice mixture all over the ribs.

5. Place the ribs on a baking sheet and cover with aluminum foil.

6. Bake for 2.5-3 hours, until the ribs are tender.

7. In a bowl, mix the barbecue sauce, apple cider vinegar, and brown sugar.

8. Remove the foil and brush the ribs with the sauce mixture.

9. Increase the oven temperature to 400¬∞F (200¬∞C) and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, until the sauce is caramelized.

10. Serve with sliced tomatoes, pickles, and coleslaw.
